Impacts of Pythagoras

Pythagoras influenced the development of mathematics and Western Philosophy which laid the groundwork for other renowned philosophers like Plato and Aristotle. His mathematical discoveries and philosophical teachings are still foundational in modern education and intellectual thought.

One of his major contributions, the Pythagorean Theorem, has many applications in real-life scenarios. Rescue workers use the Pythagorean Theorem to find out the length of their ladders, because when placed against a wall, the ladder, wall, and ground form a right triangle, making it possible for the Pythagorean Theorem to be applied. Construction workers and architects use the Pythagorean Theorem to create right angles in order to keep the building foundation stable, and miners use the Pythagorean Theorem to find out their drilling course.

Aside from his involvement in mathematics, he promoted the belief in the immortality of the soul and the idea of reincarnation, influencing Greek religious thought. Pythagoras influenced number theory, exploring concepts like perfect numbers. In music theory, he studied the mathematical relationships in musical intervals. His philosophical ideas emphasized the role of numbers and harmony in understanding the universe.

These contributions shaped Greek philosophy and impacted later scientific and mathematical thought. His work helped link mathematics with natural phenomena and harmony.
